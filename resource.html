DEFINITION

A child can be described as a slow learner if their thinking skills develop at a notably slower rate than that of their peers, and they typically have below-average intelligence. Slow learners tend to learn slower and are unable to retain what they learn, and they have a very short attention span. Learning does not happen incidentally for slow learners and they have to be directly taught. They cannot link one learning to another unless specifically told
